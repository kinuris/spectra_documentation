1. Introduction & Purpose

This Service Level Agreement (SLA) outlines the commitments of the Spectre Clothing e-commerce platform provider (hereinafter "Provider") to its users and stakeholders (hereinafter "Client") regarding system availability, performance, and support. The purpose of this SLA is to establish clear expectations and ensure a high quality of service.

Effective Date: May 31, 2025

Review Period: Annually

2. Scope of Services

This SLA applies to the core Spectre Clothing e-commerce platform, including:

  • Product browsing and purchasing functionalities.
  • User account management.
  • Order processing and tracking.
  • Inventory management system (for internal users).
  • Supplier management portal (for internal users).
  • Analytics and reporting dashboard (for internal users).

This SLA does not cover third-party integrations or services beyond the direct control of the Provider (e.g., payment gateways, shipping provider APIs), although the Provider will make reasonable efforts to ensure smooth interaction with these services.

3. Service Availability

The Provider commits to maintaining high availability for the Spectre Clothing platform.

Service Component Target Uptime Measurement Period Exclusions
Customer-Facing Storefront 99.9% Monthly Scheduled Maintenance, Force Majeure
Admin & Internal Dashboards 99.5% Monthly Scheduled Maintenance, Force Majeure

Scheduled Maintenance: The Provider will notify the Client at least 48 hours in advance of any scheduled maintenance. Maintenance windows will typically be during off-peak hours (e.g., 1:00 AM - 4:00 AM local time) and will not exceed 4 hours per month unless otherwise agreed.

4. System Performance & Response Times

The Provider commits to maintaining optimal system performance for a satisfactory user experience.

Product Page Load < 2 seconds Server-side + Client-side monitoring
Add to Cart / Checkout Steps < 3 seconds Server-side + Client-side monitoring

5. Support Services

The Provider will offer technical support for issues related to the Spectre Clothing platform.

5.1. Support Channels & Availability

  • Helpdesk Portal: Available 24/7 for ticket submission.
  • Email Support: support@spectreclothing.com - Monitored during business hours.
  • Phone Support (for Critical Issues): Available during extended business hours.

Business Hours: Monday - Friday, 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM (Local Time)

Extended Business Hours (for Critical Issues): Monday - Sunday, 7:00 AM - 10:00 PM (Local Time)

5.2. Support Response & Resolution Times

Issue Priority Definition Target Initial Response Time Target Resolution Time
Critical (P1) System outage, major functionality unavailable, significant business impact. 1 hour (within extended business hours) 4 hours
High (P2) Significant functionality impaired, moderate business impact, no workaround. 4 business hours 1 business day
Medium (P3) Minor functionality impaired, low business impact, workaround available. 1 business day 3 business days
Low (P4) General inquiries, feature requests, cosmetic issues. 2 business days As per development schedule

Initial Response Time: Time taken to acknowledge the issue and assign a support resource.

Resolution Time: Time taken to resolve the issue or provide a viable workaround. Complex issues may require longer resolution times, which will be communicated.

6. Client Responsibilities

To ensure the effective provision of services, the Client is expected to:

  • Provide accurate and timely information when reporting issues.
  • Cooperate with the Provider's support personnel in diagnosing and resolving issues.
  • Ensure their own systems and internet connectivity are functioning correctly.
  • Adhere to the terms of use of the Spectre Clothing platform.

7. Provider Responsibilities

The Provider is responsible for:

  • Meeting the service levels defined in this SLA.
  • Proactively monitoring system performance and availability.
  • Implementing security measures to protect platform and data integrity.
  • Communicating planned maintenance and unplanned outages in a timely manner.
  • Providing regular reports on SLA performance upon request.

8. SLA Exclusions

This SLA does not apply to service interruptions or performance issues resulting from:

  • Force Majeure events (e.g., natural disasters, acts of war, widespread internet outages).
  • Client-side issues (e.g., local network problems, browser incompatibilities not specified as supported, misuse of the platform).
  • Failures of third-party services not directly controlled by the Provider.
  • Activities outside the scope of defined services.
  • Denial of Service (DoS) or Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks, though the Provider will make reasonable efforts to mitigate such attacks.

9. SLA Review and Reporting

This SLA will be reviewed annually by the Provider and Client representatives. The Provider will make SLA performance reports available to the Client on a quarterly basis, or as otherwise agreed.

Note: This document is a proposed Service Level Agreement. Specific terms, conditions, and remedies for SLA breaches would typically be detailed in a legally binding contract between Spectre Clothing and its clients or defined by internal operational standards.
